# Pneumococcal Vaccination Guideline

> This guideline covers pneumococcal vaccination decisions for patients on complement
> inhibitor therapy. Decisions branch first on prior pneumococcal vaccination history,
> then by the specific prior vaccine received, then by patient age.

## Decision: Has the patient previously received any pneumococcal conjugate vaccine?

### Pathway A — No or unknown prior pneumococcal vaccination

#### Age ≤5 years

**Recommendation:**

CDC recommends routine pneumococcal vaccination for all children younger than 5 years old.

Administer a 4-dose PCV series, such as Vaxneuvance (PCV15) or Prevnar 20 (PCV20), 1 dose at each of the following ages: 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, and 12 through 15 months.

**Note:** If they received Prevnar 20 (PCV20), then they are all set, and no further vaccination is required.

If the patient received Vaxneuvance (PCV15) or Prevnar 13 (PCV13), then they should receive 1 dose of PCV20 or 1 dose of PPSV23 at least 8 weeks after the most recent PCV dose. If Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) is chosen, then they should receive a second dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) five years after the first Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose.

**Sources:**

- CDC — Pneumococcal vaccine recommendations: Infants and children: <https://www.cdc.gov/pneumococcal/hcp/vaccine-recommendations/index.html#cdc_generic_section_2-infants-and-children>
- CDC — Risk-based pneumococcal recommendations: Children 2 through 5 years: <https://www.cdc.gov/pneumococcal/hcp/vaccine-recommendations/risk-indications.html#cdc_generic_section_2-children-2-through-5-years-old>
- CDC ACIP — Pneumococcal vaccine recommendations (authoritative): <https://www.cdc.gov/acip-recs/hcp/vaccine-specific/pneumococcal.html>

#### Age 6–18 years

**Recommendation:**

If the person has not previously received any dose of Prevnar 13 (PCV13), Vaxneuvance (PCV15), or Prevnar 20 (PCV20): administer 1 dose of Vaxneuvance (PCV15) or Prevnar 20 (PCV20).

If Vaxneuvance (PCV15) is used and no previous receipt of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23), administer 1 dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) at least 8 weeks after the Vaxneuvance (PCV15) dose.

If Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) is used, administer either Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or dose 2 Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) at least 5 years after dose 1 Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23).

**Sources:**

#### Age ≥19 years

**Recommendation:**

Administer Vaxneuvance (PCV15), Prevnar 20 (PCV20), or CAPVAXIVE (PCV21) for all adults who have never received any pneumococcal conjugate vaccine or whose previous vaccination history is unknown.

Important:

- If Vaxneuvance (PCV15) is used, also administer a dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) with a minimum interval of 8 weeks after the first vaccine in adults receiving complement inhibitor therapy.
- If Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or CAPVAXIVE (PCV21) is used, a Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose isn't indicated. Regardless of whether Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or CAPVAXIVE (PCV21) is used, the pneumococcal vaccination series is complete.
- **Regional variations:** Serotype 4, which is included in Vaxneuvance (PCV15), Prevnar 20 (PCV20), and Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23), is not included in CAPVAXIVE (PCV21).
- **IMPORTANT:** Serotype 4 invasive pneumococcal disease has recently reemerged as a cause of invasive pneumococcal disease in certain regions, particularly the western United States, namely Alaska, the Navajo Nation, Colorado, New Mexico, and Oregon (CDC Active Bacterial Core surveillance, unpublished data, 2022). In those regions, vaccines other than CAPVAXIVE (PCV21) may be used preferentially.

Affected persons at risk for serotype 4 invasive pneumococcal disease are typically adults aged <65 years with a risk condition.

### Pathway B — Prior pneumococcal vaccination (known vaccine)

#### Prior vaccine: PCV7 (Prevnar)

##### Age ≤5 years

**Recommendation:**

You indicated the patient was ≤5 years old and received Prevnar 7 (PCV7). It is unlikely that this patient has received Prevnar 7 (PCV7). Prevnar 7 (PCV7) was replaced by Prevnar 13 (PCV13) in 2010 and is no longer manufactured or recommended for vaccination. Please check your data entry for accuracy.

**Sources:**

- CDC ACIP — Pneumococcal vaccine recommendations (authoritative): <https://www.cdc.gov/acip-recs/hcp/vaccine-specific/pneumococcal.html>

##### Age 6–19 years

**Recommendation:**

Give 1 dose Vaxneuvance (PCV15) or Prevnar 20 (PCV20) at least 8 weeks after the most recent pneumococcal vaccine, regardless of prior Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) or Prevnar 7 (PCV7). If Prevnar 20 (PCV20) is used, vaccinations are complete. But if Vaxneuvance (PCV15) is used, follow with Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) eight weeks later, if not previously given.

If Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) is given before Vaxneuvance (PCV15), the recommendation is to wait one year before giving Vaxneuvance (PCV15). Some experts wait just eight weeks in high-risk patients.

If Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) is used, administer either Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or the second dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) at least 5 years after the first dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23).

#### Prior vaccine: PCV13 (Prevnar 13) only

**Recommendation:**

- If the patient has received only 3 doses: Give 1 dose Vaxneuvance (PCV15) or Prevnar 20 (PCV20) at least 8 weeks after the most recent dose.

- Administer 1 dose of Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or 1 dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) at least 8 weeks after the most recent Prevnar 13 (PCV13). If Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) is used, administer 1 dose of Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or the second dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) at least 5 years after the first dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23).

#### Prior vaccine: PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23)

##### Age ≤5 years

**Recommendation:**

Patients aged 2–5 years on complement inhibitor therapy who have previously received Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) should receive either Prevnar 13 (PCV13), Vaxneuvance (PCV15), or Prevnar 20 (PCV20) at least 8 weeks after the Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose to complete their recommended vaccination series.

For children in this age group with immunocompromising conditions who completed their recommended PCV series after receiving Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23), the subsequent recommendations depend on whether Prevnar 20 (PCV20) was administered. If the child previously received at least one dose of Prevnar 20 (PCV20), no further pneumococcal vaccinations are needed. Their vaccination is complete.

If Prevnar 20 (PCV20) was not previously received, one dose of Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or one dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) should be administered at least 8 weeks after the most recent PCV dose. If Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) is chosen instead of Prevnar 20 (PCV20), a second dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) should be given at least 5 years after the first Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose.

Patients aged ≥2 years on complement inhibitor therapy should receive a second dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) at least 5 years after the first Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose if they did not receive Prevnar 20 (PCV20) to complete their series. This revaccination recommendation reflects the increased and sustained risk of invasive pneumococcal disease in immunocompromised populations, including those on complement inhibitor therapy.

For children aged 24–71 months with any risk condition (including complement inhibitor therapy) who have not completed their PCV series, two doses of Prevnar 13 (PCV13) or Vaxneuvance (PCV15) are recommended with an interval of at least 8 weeks between doses. This applies regardless of prior Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23), ensuring that immunocompromised children receive adequate priming with conjugate vaccines.

##### Age 6–19 years

**Recommendation:**

Patients on complement inhibitor therapy who are older than 5 years and have previously received Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) should receive a single dose of Vaxneuvance (PCV15) or Prevnar 20 (PCV20) at least 1 year after their last Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose.

For patients aged 6–18 years on complement inhibitor therapy who previously received Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23), the recommendation is to administer either Vaxneuvance (PCV15) or Prevnar 20 (PCV20) at least 8 weeks after the Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose. If Prevnar 20 (PCV20) was not received, one dose of Prevnar 20 (PCV20) should be administered at least 8 weeks after the most recent pneumococcal vaccine. If, instead of Prevnar 20 (PCV20), a second dose of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) is given, then this second dose of PPSV23 should be given at least 5 years after the first Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose.

##### Age ≥19 years

**Recommendation:**

If your patient previously received only Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23), ACIP recommends a single dose of Vaxneuvance (PCV15), Prevnar 20 (PCV20), or CAPVAXIVE (PCV21) at least 1 year after the last Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) dose. If PCV15 is used in this age group, no additional PPSV23 doses are recommended, completing the pneumococcal vaccination series.

For those aged 65 years or older who previously received both Prevnar 13 (PCV13) and Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23) (with PPSV23 administered at age ≥65 years), shared clinical decision-making is recommended regarding administration of either a single dose of Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or CAPVAXIVE (PCV21) at least 5 years after the last pneumococcal vaccine dose.

If Prevnar 20 (PCV20) or CAPVAXIVE (PCV21) is administered, the pneumococcal vaccination series is complete and does not require additional pneumococcal vaccine doses, regardless of age or prior receipt of Pneumovax 23 (PPSV23).
