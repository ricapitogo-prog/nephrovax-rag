# Haemophilus influenzae Type B (Hib) Vaccination Guideline

> This guideline covers Hib vaccination decisions for patients on complement inhibitor
> therapy. Decisions branch on prior Hib vaccination history and patient age.

## Decision: Has the patient previously received any Haemophilus influenzae type B vaccine?

### Pathway A — No or unknown prior Hib vaccination

#### Age <5 years

**Recommendation:**

All patients <5 years old who are on complement inhibitor therapy should receive the routine schedule of the 4-dose (or 3-dose) series at 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, and at 12–15 months (booster).

Vaccine Options include:

- ActHIB, Hiberix, Pentacel, or Vaxelis: 4-dose series.
  - *Note: Vaxelis not recommended for the booster dose.*
- PedvaxHIB: 3-dose series at 2, 4, and 12–15 months.

According to ACIP, for American Indian/Alaska Native infants, Vaxelis and PedvaxHIB are preferred for the primary series.

Additional special guidance for patients on complement inhibitor therapy:

- If the patient is unvaccinated OR has only received 1 dose before age 12 months, then administer 2 doses, 8 weeks apart.
- If the patient has received 2 or more doses before age 12 months, then administer 1 dose at least 8 weeks after the previous dose.

**Note:** Hib monovalent and combination conjugate vaccines are inactivated vaccines and may be administered to persons with immunocompromising conditions, though the immunologic response to the vaccine might be suboptimal.

For patients receiving additional immunosuppressive therapy in addition to complement inhibitor therapy, special timing considerations apply (given possible suboptimal immunologic response). Patients vaccinated within 14 days of starting immunosuppressive therapy, or while receiving immunosuppressive therapy, should be considered unimmunized, and doses should be repeated at least 3 months after completion of immunosuppressive therapy.

#### Age ≥5 years

**Recommendation:**

For unimmunized older children, adolescents, and adults on complement inhibitor therapy, a single dose of any licensed Hib conjugate vaccine is recommended.

### Pathway B — Prior Hib vaccination

#### Age <5 years

**Recommendation:**

You have indicated that your patient is <5 years old and has received prior Haemophilus influenzae Type B (Hib) vaccination. If your patient has completed the series outlined below, they are all set.

- If the patient has only received 1 dose before age 12 months, then administer 2 doses, 8 weeks apart.
- If the patient has received 2 or more doses before age 12 months, then administer 1 dose at least 8 weeks after the previous dose.

Complete series schedule:

All patients <5 years old who are on complement inhibitor therapy should receive the routine schedule of the 4-dose (or 3-dose) series at 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, and at 12–15 months (booster).

Vaccine Options include:

- ActHIB, Hiberix, Pentacel, or Vaxelis: 4-dose series.
  - *Note: Vaxelis not recommended for the booster dose.*
- PedvaxHIB: 3-dose series (2, 4, and 12–15 months).

According to ACIP, for American Indian/Alaska Native infants, Vaxelis and PedvaxHIB are preferred for the primary series.

#### Age ≥5 years

**Recommendation:**

A single dose of any licensed Hib conjugate vaccine is recommended for patients who have never been vaccinated and who are receiving complement inhibitor therapy. If your patient has received one dose after age 5, they are all set.

If your patient was vaccinated before the age of 5, then they should have received the complete series according to the following schedule:

All patients <5 years old who are on complement inhibitor therapy should receive the routine schedule of the 4-dose (or 3-dose) series at 2 months, 4 months, 6 months, and at 12–15 months (booster).

Vaccine Options include:

- ActHIB, Hiberix, Pentacel, or Vaxelis: 4-dose series.
  - *Note: Vaxelis not recommended for the booster dose.*
- PedvaxHIB: 3-dose series (2, 4, and 12–15 months).

According to ACIP, for American Indian/Alaska Native infants, Vaxelis and PedvaxHIB are preferred for the primary series.

Additional special guidance for patients on complement inhibitor therapy:

- If the patient is unvaccinated OR has only received 1 dose before age 12 months, then administer 2 doses, 8 weeks apart.
- If the patient has received 2 or more doses before age 12 months, then administer 1 dose at least 8 weeks after the previous dose.
