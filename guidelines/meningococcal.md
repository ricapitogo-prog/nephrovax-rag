# Meningococcal Vaccination Guideline

> This guideline covers meningococcal vaccination decisions for patients on complement
> inhibitor therapy. Decisions branch first on prior meningococcal vaccination history,
> then by the specific prior vaccine received, then by patient age. Patients aged
> ≥10 years on complement inhibitor therapy require both MenACWY and MenB coverage.

## Decision: Has the patient previously received any meningococcal vaccine?

### Pathway A — No or unknown prior meningococcal vaccination

#### Age <2 years (2–23 months)

**Recommendation:**

The following vaccines are recommended for patients aged <2 years on complement inhibitor therapy:

MenACWY only (MenB vaccines are not part of the ACIP recommendations for this age group).

Menveo (MenACWY-CRM):

- When initiating at age 2 months: 4-dose series at 2, 4, 6, and 12 months.
- When initiating at age 3–6 months: 3- or 4-dose series (dose 2 [and dose 3 if applicable] at least 8 weeks after previous dose until a dose is received at age 7 months or older, followed by an additional dose at least 12 weeks later and after age 12 months).
- When initiating at age 7–23 months: 2-dose series with second dose in second year of life, at least 3 months after first dose.

#### Age 2–9 years

**Recommendation:**

MenACWY only (MenB vaccines are not part of the ACIP recommendations for this age group).

Options include:

- MenACWY-CRM (MENVEO), or
- MenACWY-TT (MENQUADFI)

*These vaccines are **interchangeable**, meaning you can switch between them for different doses if needed.*

Primary Vaccination (the same for both options):

- 2 doses ≥8 weeks apart.

Booster Schedule (the same for both options):

- If the patient is <7 years old: Single dose 3 years after primary, then every 5 years while on a complement inhibitor.
- If the patient is ≥7 years old: Single dose 5 years after primary, then every 5 years while on a complement inhibitor.

#### Age ≥10 years

**Recommendation:**

Patients 10 years and older on complement inhibitor therapy are recommended to receive both vaccines: MenACWY AND MenB.

MenACWY Options include:

- MenACWY-CRM (MENVEO), or
- MenACWY-TT (MENQUADFI)

*These vaccines are interchangeable.*

MenB Options:

- MenB-FHbp (TRUMENBA), or
- MenB-4C (BEXSERO)

*These are NOT interchangeable—the patient must complete the series with the same product.*

Alternatively the patient can use combined vaccine options that include MenACWY AND MenB in the same vaccine:

- MenACWY-TT/MenB-FHbp (PENBRAYA)
- MenACWY-CRM/MenB-4C (PENMENVY)

CDC recommends MenACWY (Serogroups A, C, W, and Y meningococcal conjugate vaccines) AND MenB (Serogroup B meningococcal vaccines) for patients ≥10 years old who are receiving a complement inhibitor. Rather than two separate vaccines, these could be combined into a single pentavalent vaccine (see above).

Note that the use of some of these vaccines in certain age groups is off-label. Please refer to the manufacturer's prescription information for on-label and approved indications.

The **MenACWY** vaccines should be administered according to the following schedule:

- Primary vaccination: Administer 2 doses ≥8 weeks apart.
- Booster vaccination: Administer a single dose at 5 years after primary vaccination and every 5 years thereafter for as long as on complement inhibitor therapy.

The **MenB** vaccines should be administered according to the following schedule:

- Primary vaccination, MenB-FHbp (Trumenba): Administer 3 doses at 0, 1–2, and 6 months, or
- Primary vaccination, MenB-4C (Bexsero): Administer 3 doses at 0, 1–2, and 6 months.
- Booster vaccination, MenB-FHbp (Trumenba) or MenB-4C (Bexsero): Administer a single dose at 1 year after completion of primary vaccination and every 2–3 years thereafter for as long as on complement inhibitor therapy.
- Note: MenB-FHbp and MenB-4C are not interchangeable.

If using the combined MenACWY-MenB vaccines MenACWY-TT/MenB-FHbp (Penbraya) or MenACWY-CRM/MenB-4C (Penmenvy):

- Primary vaccination: Administer 2 doses 6 months apart.
- Booster vaccination: Administer a single dose at 1 year after completion of primary vaccination and every 2–3 years thereafter for as long as on complement inhibitor therapy.

If your patient completed the combined MenACWY-TT/MenB-FHbp (Penbraya) or MenACWY-CRM/MenB-4C (Penmenvy) vaccination series (2 doses 6 months apart), then they should receive the booster every 5 years for the MenACWY component and every 2–3 years for the MenB component. The boosters can be combined vaccines when both are due, but often need to use individual and compatible vaccines given different booster schedules for MenACWY (booster every 5 years) and MenB (booster every 2–3 years) vaccines.

While MenACWY vaccines are interchangeable, MenB vaccines are not; therefore, MenB boosters must be of the same type as the initial vaccine.

For example, if Penbraya (MenACWY-TT/MenB-FHbp) was used for the initial vaccine, then the MenB booster in 2–3 years should be TRUMENBA (MenB-FHbp), while the MenACWY vaccine in 5 years can be MenACWY-CRM (MENVEO) or MenACWY-TT (MENQUADFI).

However, if Penmenvy (MenACWY-CRM/MenB-4C) was used as the initial vaccine, then the MenB booster in 2–3 years should be BEXSERO (MenB-4C), while the MenACWY vaccine in 5 years can be MenACWY-CRM (MENVEO) or MenACWY-TT (MENQUADFI).

##### Age ≥10 years

**Recommendation:**

If the primary series with MenACWY is complete, follow the booster schedule for MenACWY:

Booster vaccination:

- Administer a single dose at 5 years after primary vaccination and every 5 years thereafter for as long as on complement inhibitor therapy.

MenACWY Options include:

- MenACWY-CRM (MENVEO), or
- MenACWY-TT (MENQUADFI)

*MenACWY vaccines are interchangeable.*

Must also add MenB series:

MenB Options:

- MenB-FHbp (TRUMENBA), or
- MenB-4C (BEXSERO)

*These are NOT interchangeable—the patient must complete the series and booster with the same product.*

The **MenB** vaccines should be administered according to the following schedule:

- Primary vaccination, MenB-FHbp (Trumenba): Administer 3 doses at 0, 1–2, and 6 months, or
- Primary vaccination, MenB-4C (BEXSERO): Administer 3 doses at 0, 1–2, and 6 months.
- Booster vaccination, MenB-FHbp (Trumenba) or MenB-4C (Bexsero): Administer a single dose at 1 year after completion of primary vaccination and every 2–3 years thereafter for as long as on complement inhibitor therapy.
